Okay… let’s clear up some confusion

“So what’s the difference between AI and Machine Learning?”

AI is the goal.
Machine Learning is one way to reach that goal.

AI is about making computers do things that look intelligent.
Machine Learning is how we teach computers — by showing them examples instead of writing rules.

Think of it like this:

AI is learning to ride a bicycle.
Machine Learning is practicing again and again until you stop falling.

“Then what’s the deal with AI and models?”

AI is the idea.
A model is the thing that actually does the work.

AI is the concept of making machines act smart.
A model is a trained system that takes input and gives output.

Another way to think about it:

AI is the idea of cooking.
A model is a specific recipe.

Some common myths about AI

“So AI thinks like a human, basically?”

Not really.
AI doesn’t think — it guesses.

It predicts what comes next based on patterns it has seen before.

Think of autocorrect… but on steroids.

DUCK → DUCKING → DUCKING HELL

“But AI understands meaning, right?”

AI only knows which words usually appear together.

Imagine someone who learned a language by reading subtitles —
they can form sentences, but they don’t truly understand them.

“AI is always right.”

Nuh uh.

AI can be confidently wrong — like that one person you know who answers fast and very, very wrong.

AI just guesses.
Sometimes, those guesses are bad.
